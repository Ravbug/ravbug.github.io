Ravbug Animations was an American computer animation studio based in California, United States. It created short webfilms published on YouTube surrounding the popular video game Minecraft. For a period of approximately four years it released videos averaging between one minute and eight minutes in length, until a lack of resources forced its shutdown in late 2018.

Production

Each episode centers takes place within a common subspace of the Minecraft universe and features a unique plot, with some characters reused across episodes. The intended audience was teenagers to young adults, with content that followed family-friendly guidelines. Unlike many other channels on YouTube, Ravbug Animations did not create content that featured multiple game franchises at once. None of the characters have official names except for the main original character (OC), who features the name of the studio.

The creation of each episode followed a typical production pipeline for animation, with some notable exceptions. After the scriptwriting process, production proceeded directly to the animation stage skipping the storyboarding and animatic phases, because of a lack of skill in drawing. In addition, animation would often begin before the plot had been finalized, leading to pacing issues. After animation was complete, the process of adding sound effects and music would begin, occurring while the final frames were being rendered. Over the years, the rendering and composition process became increasingly complex, while the sound and music processes remained relatively static.

Ravbug Animations created its own assets for use in its productions. For environments, a tool named Mineways was used which could read Minecraft world files and convert them into Wavefront OBJ triangle meshes. None of the episodes feature original music or sound effects, instead each episode relied on royalty-free music and sound effects packs. In later years, some of the original assets used in the productions became available for free download on the studio's website. Asset reuse was a common practice, with entire environments appearing in multiple unrelated episodes.

Keyframe animation was the primary animation technique, however, some episodes also leveraged motion capture technology to save time animating crowd characters and backgrounds. The studio acquired a used Xbox 360 Kinect sensor for free, which served as an effective and accessible method for incorporating motion capture.

The studio constantly struggled with time and resources. The only income came from Google AdSense on videos, which did not provide enough funding for additional staff members. As of 2019, the studio had ceased production of all animated content, and its parent organization focused on other ventures, like game development.

The studio attempted multiple times to create a series of videos within the same premise, but due to the significant time investment required for the production of an animation, none of these series made it past two episodes. These series were:
  • Mob Shenanigans
  • The Adventures of The Wolf and the Ocelot
After the conclusion of both of these series, the studio focused on individual self-contained episodes.

Cast The episodes feature some recurring characters:

  • Ravbug, the only character with a name, and designed to resemble the studio's sole employee
  • A wolf
  • An ocelot
  • Villagers, which are used interchangeably and do not have distinct personalities
In addition, the episodes feature numerous cameos to other YouTube creators, other franchises like Bob The Builder, and as some of the employee's friends.

Reception

The films were generally well-received by audiences on YouTube, who praised the style, humor, visual effects, and writing. Some reviewers were more critical of the long delays between episodes and noticeable animation errors. In later years, a few viewers expressed nostalgia for the videos in the comments sections on YouTube.
