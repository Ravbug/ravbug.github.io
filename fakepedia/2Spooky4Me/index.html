2Spooky4Me is an American animated web series created by Clay God. It is based on the horror video game series Five Nights at Freddy's and follows the player's experience within each game. The series premiered on YouTube and ran for four episodes before its cancellation.

Premise

Each episode centers around a different installment of the Five Nights at Freddy's game series, and begins focused on the main character. The main character typically looks at his security camera screen before being "jumpscared" by an animatronic, a type of main character in Five Nights at Freddy's games. Afterward, the episode explores how the game the episode is based on proves the existence of the Illuminati. Each episode also contains a reference to the series' name 2Spooky4Me, though this reference varies per episode (2 Spooky 4 me, 2 Spooky 5 me, etc). The episodes end with a request that the viewer subscribe, sometimes accompanied by a vague threat for if the viewer does not.

Later episodes deviate from the formula established in the first two episodes.

Production

The series is notable for its low budget, with an estimated per-episode cost of $0. Despite the budget constraints, Clay God produced all of the episodes in-house at its studio in the United States. The series makes aggressive use of timesaving techniques in its animation and sound design, such as fixed cameras, royalty-free music, and text-to-speech for voice acting. Production also used freely available software such as iMovie for editing and PicMonkey for the artwork.

The series features a 2D hand-drawn art style, though cell animation was not used. Instead, the artist manually created each full frame by subtly changing the previous frame and exporting a new image. These images were then sequenced in the editing software to produce a video and later add a soundtrack. The technique is similar to straight-ahead animation, but this particular workflow made it difficult revise a previous frame.

The first episode was released in 4:3 SD resolution, though subsequent episodes were released in HD widescreen.

Reception

The series was well-received by audiences on YouTube, who praised the style, humor, and writing.

Availability

At present, the series is unavailable. A few years after the final episode's release, the series was made private on YouTube.

Re-release

It is unknown if Clay God will re-publish the episodes, though some fans are hopeful.

Revival

Clay God has stated that he currently does not have plans to continue the series.
